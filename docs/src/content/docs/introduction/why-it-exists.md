You pick a starter. You catch whatever looks cool in the first two routes. Twenty hours later you've got three Water types, no way to hit a Ground wall, and the Elite Four is two towns away. Starting Six is the thing I wished existed before I committed to the next playthrough — a place to plan the full six, check the coverage, and confirm the team actually works before I sink the time in.

## What it solves

- **Type coverage gaps**: a Gen 9 18x18 effectiveness matrix runs over your team and flags shared weaknesses, uncovered defensive types, and offensive holes.
- **Move pool visibility**: browse each Pokemon's full learnset so you can see what a slot can actually do, not just what it is.
- **Pre-commitment planning**: assign four moves, an ability, and a Tera type per slot before the run starts, so you're not rerolling encounters mid-playthrough.
- **Per-game context**: playthroughs are tied to a specific game, so the dex filter matches what you can actually catch.

## Who this is for

Players who like to plan a bit before they play. If you enjoy spreadsheeting a team, comparing learnsets, or working out Tera picks ahead of the first gym, this fits. If you speedrun, nuzlocke with strict rules engines, or just vibe through a playthrough catching whatever you want, you probably don't need it. It's not a competitive battle simulator either — no damage calc, no EV/IV optimizer, no ladder scraping.
